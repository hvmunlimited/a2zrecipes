The recipe editor is the main way for you to enter recipes into your cookbook. The editor contains four sections, and you can switch between them by using the menu on the left. (On larger screens, all four sections may be on the screen at once.)

The first section, "Recipe Information", is where you can edit the general information about a recipe. Here you can give your recipe a name, write a brief description, and note how long the recipe will take, among other things. Except for the name, everything is optional, though the app expects you to at least try to fill as many fields as you can. (For example, you don't have to enter the time for your recipes, but, if you do, you can use the "time search" function later.)

The second section is a simple list of ingredients. You can touch and hold an ingredient that is already in the list to remove it or edit it. Also, note that the app does not check your ingredients in any way, though later versions will offer a "search by ingredient" function.

Directions or steps go in the third section. Again, this is a simple list, but with a twist. Because the order of steps is important for a recipe, the touch-and-hold menu allows you to move individual steps up and down in the list.

The fourth section is where you can tag a recipe. You can use as many tags as you like, and they can be named whatever you wish. (If you tag your recipes consistantly, then the "search by tag" function will be very helpful if you have a large cookbook.)

Finally, each recipe can have an associated picture. This can be any image on your device, and the chosen image will appear with the recipe in the viewer and flipbook display modes.

When you're done, hit the "Save" option, and your recipe will be saved into your cookbook.
